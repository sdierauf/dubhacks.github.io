DubHacks is the largest hackathon of the Northwest

For students by students


The event will host the top college hackers in the northwest, gathered to build the next generation of innovative software and hardware products. Join us!

FAQ

Who can attend?

All students from any university can attend. Apply with your .edu email address and you are ready to go. If you do not fit in this category, feel free to contact us.

When is DubHacks?

The hackathon will be held in Fall 2014. The gates will open on Friday, October 17th at 5:00pm and continue onto the following day until 8:00 p.m. when the prizes will be given out. For those who wish to arrive earlier in the week, there will be workshops as a build up to the final hackathon.

What should I bring?

All your hack gear. Passion, keyboard, mouse, laptop and other necessary hardware (don't forget your chargers!). Along with that you are free to bring toiletries, sleeping bags and a change of clothes. You should bring any valid form of identification and your ticket for security purposes.

How much does this event cost?

Absolutely free. Make sure to bring your friends!

How big are teams?

A team can be as big as 5 people or could consist of a couple of people. It is not necessary that you form your teams before the hackathon. In fact it would be better if you network around and make your final team at that time.

How do I get there?

If you live in the NorthWest area such as Portland or Vancouver, then bussing is a very good option. If you live outside this area, then send an email to contact@dubhacks.co and we will give more details on how we might be able to help. The location of the hackathon is at the University of Washington HUB building: The Husky Union Building (HUB)

What should I build?

Anything really. The project is up to you and your team. You have 24 hours to make something, anything from the web, desktop apps, mobile apps or even traditional software.

Are we allowed to build on past projects?

A hackathon is an experience to build on something completely new within a set amount of time. With that in mind, you cannot work on past projects. Using your own API's or Third Party API's is alright as long as the project is a fresh start.

I have a different question. Who can I ask?

You can send an email across to contact@dubhacks.co, and we will get back to you as soon as we can.

DubHacks is the first major collegiate hackathon in the Pacific North West.

This October 17-18, over 300 top college hackers in the northwest will gather at The HUB at the UW to build the next generation of innovative software and hardware hacks.

  • Mentorship for everyone
  • Hardware hacking equipment
  • Buses to/from nearby universities
  • Tons of food, drinks, events

FAQ

Who can attend?

We strongly encourage undergraduate students from any University to attend DubHacks. If you do not fit in this category, feel free to contact us.

When is DubHacks?

The gates will open on Friday, October 17th at 5:00pm and continue onto the following day until 8:00 p.m. when the prizes will be given out. Full schedule will be posted soon.

What should I bring?

All your hack gear. Passion, keyboard, mouse, laptop and other necessary hardware (don't forget your chargers!). Along with that you are free to bring toiletries, sleeping bags and a change of clothes. You should bring any valid form of identification and your ticket for security purposes.

How much does this event cost?

Absolutely free. We will provide your WiFi, meals, caffeine, swag, and the workspace.

How big are teams?

A team can be as big as 5 people or could consist of a couple of people. You can do it alone, but it'll be less fun!

How do I get there?

If you live in the NorthWest area such as Portland or Vancouver, then bussing is a very good option. If you live outside this area, then send an email to contact@dubhacks.co and we will give more details on how we might be able to help. The location of the hackathon is at the University of Washington, HUB building: The Husky Union Building (HUB)

What should I build?

Anything really. The project is up to you and your team. You have 24 hours to make something, anything from the web, desktop apps, mobile apps or even traditional software.

Are we allowed to build on past projects?

A hackathon is an experience to build on something completely new within a set amount of time. With that in mind, you cannot work on past projects. Using your own API's or Third Party API's is alright as long as the project is a fresh start.

Will you have hardware prototyping equipment?

Yes. Our partners at MLH will have limited stock of Oculus Rift, Myo arm-band, Leap Motion, Arduino and more to give out to hackers for the duration of the hackathon.

I have a different question. Who can I ask?

Please feel free to reach out to us via Facebook, Twitter, or send an email to contact@dubhacks.co, and we will get back to you as soon as we can.
